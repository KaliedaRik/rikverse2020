(After Federico García Lorca's El poeta pregunta a su amor por la «Ciudad Encantada» de Cuenca)

You liked the city that by drop on drop
the water carved amid the round of pines?
You saw, did you, the dreams and faces, roads
and walls, those walls of pain the air there whips?

And you saw the blue crack, the broken moon
the Júcar soaks in crystals and their trills?
Your fingers, did their tips then kiss the thorns
that weave in love to crown the distant stone?

Did you remember me as you then climbed
towards those silent jags of rock and snakes,
a prisoner of crickets and soft shades?

Did you not spot beyond the clean, clear air
a dahlia of heartbreaks stitched with joy
that my hot heart commanded up to you?
