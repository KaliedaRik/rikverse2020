(After Andrei Voznesensky's Торгуют арбузами)

Moscow is jam-packed with watermelons.
All breathe free and unbounded.
And unbridled power blusters
from staccatto stall-criers.

Awnings. Uproar. Scarved girls.
They laugh. They clatter their change.

Knives and ace-high cuts.
Hey, Guv'nor, no worries!

Who wants a watermelon?
It's verging to split! –
And so juicy and so tasty
And the militia's cap cloth
And the wall-stacked scooters.

The September air is sharp to the tongue
And just as ringing as the melons.

And so cheerful and homespun,
Like that watermelon at the gate,
The Earth wobbles
In a string-bag
Of meridians and latitudes.
