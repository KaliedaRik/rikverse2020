Hail the spore as it kisses the ant!
Watch as it sprouts and mounts its steed:

white threads needle over the barding;
sharp tips lance the pauldron gaps.

Now in, it spins and knits a new flesh
between the silks of muscle and fat.

A circlet of barbs surround, siege the bascinet
ganglia, and breach: fresh rules for the beast.

Come a new dawn, a penitent ant clambers
high over scaffolds to view England's heart;

it lifts its belly to salute the dressed crowds
as they scurry from train to stall to concrete cell.

When the Grail erupts from its annealed armour
a billion spores puff over us: Camelot blooms!
