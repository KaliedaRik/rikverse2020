This miniature version of man
who has taken my storyline
and spliced it with chapters
from a woman I love takes me
for an adventure.

"We're in deep danger now: there's theives
in these woods — pirates even! Or wolves!"

We beat through brambles and scuffle
dry leaves, their poisons mulching
the air we breathe. Toadstools
dust boots with spores, a trail
for bloodhounds to sneeze across
as they hunt the wrongly accused.

"Watch out, Dad — oh, that's real gross!
If that was a landmine, you'd be dead!"

My foot breaks the ribs of a squirrel, the space
where its eyes once rested accusing me. The remains
of its guts drip grubs to the ground, seeking escape
from exposure. They piston their flesh into the earth
eager to straitjacket desperate animations and forge
their gel juices into the freedoms of flies.

And my lover's eyes borrowed by my son
watch me grimace, shake offal from my boot:

"You smile funny when you step in shit —
Mum says you want to be cremated, like Nana;
did they burn her flowers too? I like roses!"

Sometimes I look at you, summer child, stunned
by the way you write your own book; the way
you rewrite mine without even bothering to ask.
