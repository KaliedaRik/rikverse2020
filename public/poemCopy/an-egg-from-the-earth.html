(After the Sutton Hoo Helm)

You've taken a beating over the years
as the hill above slumped and leached

a skull from your tight caress; your story,
face-stamped, needs no jaw to relate:

here be dragons! Was your Bretwalda
worthy of your coppered plates? He made

you his crown and took you to his ship set
cold above the waves, snug in its clay.

I dare not stare: your sockets demand eyes
fill them, let boars snuffle across brows.
