To the cat on his back who sulks
for the lack of some verse he can tack
to his name: shall I jig as you nip
at my toes? Believe me, dear puss,
that the lengths I would go to achieve
an award of your purr are beyond
the measure of claws; do I dare
an attack on your belly-flared fur?
The unblinking attention of bronzed
glare suggests – no. Perhaps it is best
if I give you some lines and retreat:
in the land of this lion, I'm lunch.
