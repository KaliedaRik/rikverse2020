"A hen roosts in the pivot of the Buddha's arm."

I see the face of a neoglyph within a mirror's frame.
The face, it flickers between a father and a mother.
A hand can clap a slideshow between relatives.
Each tap of the cheek resolves to a new memory.
Who owns this face? Each morning I confront that face:
are you me? Today's morning, I smell a char of feathers.
My brother is smoke – five years blown apart.
Faces lie. The face is not my brother. Tap ... tap.
This face I could love. I could cuddle its curves
in the crook of an arm, kiss its cool lips.
Wear it out. Tap ... tap.
