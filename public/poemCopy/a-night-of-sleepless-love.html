That night and us aloft with the full moon:
it brought me to tears; to you it raised laughs.
Your scorn seemed Godlike, while my grievances
no more than small splinters and doves in chains.

That night and us beneath. Crystal sorrow
brought you to tears for such deep distances.
My anguish formed a harsh cluster of pains
around your weak and sand embedded heart.

The dawn drew us together to the bed,
our mouths set as one to the frozen stream
of a wound without end that pours, that spills.

And the sun broke the shuttered balcony
and the living coral spread wide its fan
around my cold and shroud embedded heart.
