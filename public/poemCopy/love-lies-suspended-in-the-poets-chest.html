Never will you know how hard I love you;

I'm crying here, now; I hide you, hunted
by a voice that penetrates even steel.

The lore that stirs both flesh and star alike,
it punctures, seeps into my chest in aches –
a stew of mulm words have bitten and chewed
the hard angle wings of your stringent soul.

People cluster in lush gardens: they jump;
they wait to see your shape, my agony;
they ride horses with manes of light and green.

So you must maintain your slumber, my life.
Hear my blood as it breaks in violins!
Can you see them? Still they are stalking us!
