(After a tree in Ierapetra, Greece)

A feather of boughs, sweet as dawnlight
above the tide line; such a frail host

for a god's coffined flesh, yet your heart
blunts the sharpest chisel and your bole

pillared a palace. Could you truly feed
the the chosen folk for forty lost years?

Who knows, a wave whispers as I watch
surfs skittle amid pebbles, let your salt breath

tang a throat. My bones hallow your saps
as they soothe my ruddy, ached joints.
