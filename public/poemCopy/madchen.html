(After the Venus of Hohle Fels)

She is, above all, a drama of curves
carved from the tooth. A finger's trophy,

she maps the wills of our sires and dams
in fragments, a mistress of ecologies

whose beasts rut-roar in powder silences –
shall daylight humble her? Shall the measure

of each of her arcs and scars strip apart
the last of our homeless, hidden humanity?

Her caverns guest only the voyeur's breath:
the stars in her heavens spin apart.
