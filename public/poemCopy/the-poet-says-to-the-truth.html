I want to weep my heart-wrecks: I say this
so you shall come to love and weep for me
one night where nightingales call to the air,
and you and dagger blade and touch and kiss.

I want to kill the only witness, see,
who saw the murder, saw my flowers die
and then I can convert my tears, my sweat
to an everlasting heap of hard wheat.

I hope the loose-spooled thread of I love you,
of you love me – it never ends: it burns
along with that old moon, the guttered sun.

This thing that you won't give, that I don't seek
from you will be for death, that death which leaves
no shadow marks across the trembling flesh.
