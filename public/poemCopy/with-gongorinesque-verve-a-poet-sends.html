This young Turian dove I send to you,
with sweet bright eyes and plumes so pale and full,
over the noble laurel pours and adds
a slow and loving flame to where I stay.

Its candid virtue, its throat of soft down,
presents a swirl of caught and heated foams
with a trembled frost, pearl and shades of mist –
it seeks to mark an absent mouth: your mouth.

Now pass your hands across its full white space
and you will see flurries of song-spun snow –
it settles on your elegance in flakes.

And thus my heart now caught by night and day
and jailed within these walls of dark-held love
must weep its harsh distress, not seeing you.
