I have a fear: a loss of wonder glimpsed –
that stare, your marbled eyes, and that light tick
your breath at night on my cheek – touch and touch
a single rose brushed in the breeze of sleep.

I hold a sorrow: stuck upon this shore
a twist of trunk, my branches polled – yet most
I have no bloom, no bark or heart or soil
to feed the grubs that feed the pain I shine.

If you alone remain my hidden jewel,
if you're my torture cross, my sweet-sprung hurt –
if I am but a dog at your command,

never let me lose this thing that I've gained
and decorate your river's deepest flow
with a sheen of leaves dropped in my lost fall.
