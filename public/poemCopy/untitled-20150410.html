When I cough too long
I see rainbow bubbles –
flying carpets for fairies
who have lost their wings

to age. When I cough
your words fall apart,
a jumble of elvish spells
shivering the air to

jack at my ears. When
I cough and cough walls
shudder and trees march;
my troll foot slips on carpet

and pixies swirl my belly …
such magic I see in the world
around, such a knot of insights
as I beg my lungs to inspire.
