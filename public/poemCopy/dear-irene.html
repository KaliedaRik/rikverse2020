(After an icon of Irene, possibly from Athens)

How sweet of your husband to die
so soon after stealing your icons.

You taught generals that the writ
of a woman has God's esteem,

that peace is worth the cost of silk;
God hides in sainted, painted gold.

Sappho herself mopped your brow
as you died amid unspun wool. Still,

the son's eyes had to be widened:
kiss the cracked gilt frame, and sleep.
