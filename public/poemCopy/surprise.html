You boxed me back in ninety three,
a crook-toothed smile slung through dim light
to hook my eyes, a scatter of limbs
lanky in their jeans and tee - not my type
at all and yet, and yet

here we are, somehow, shook together
so hard by that life we built I dare not guess
where you end, I start - a melt of cheese
over toast, cream and crunch, a gift
to the world and still, and still.
