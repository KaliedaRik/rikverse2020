The arch of the branch
drips dew from bud to crocus:
a swallow shivers.

This waterfall roars –
fish released from icy pools
caught amid the spray.

Mud on the fingers
crumbles to beads as they rub:
seeds mixed in dead skin.

Boots in the high street: dash
and stop and dash on concrete –
a weed in the crack.

The first bee wanders,
a cold flight through frosty air:
this petal unfurls.
